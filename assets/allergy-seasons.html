Season By Season

What Peaks When

Trees, grasses, weeds, and year-round triggers all move through one continuous cycle here in Central Texas. This keeps the page reading like one system with clear headers for each part.

Image Placeholder

Trees

Trees dominate the Central Texas allergy calendar from December through June. Cedar opens the cycle in winter, then oak, mulberry, pecan, and other spring trees keep symptoms going well into early summer.

Grasses

Grasses take over from spring into summer. Unlike tree pollen, grass exposure is everywhere in Central Texas, from lawns and sports fields to roadsides and open lots.

Weeds

After the brief midsummer lull, weed pollen drives late summer and fall symptoms. Ragweed leads the charge, but several other weeds extend that second allergy season across Central Texas.

Always Present

All Year

These triggers do not wait for a season change. They stay in the background every month and often make spring or fall flares feel even worse.

Mold Spores
Moderate · Year-Round
JFMAMJ JASOND

Mold spores thrive in warm, humid conditions, which Central Texas has plenty of. Indoor mold stays active year-round, while outdoor mold spikes after rain and through the warm months.

Dust Mites
Moderate · Year-Round
JFMAMJ JASOND

Dust mites live in bedding, carpet, and upholstery. They peak in warm humid weather, but they never fully disappear, which is why symptoms often feel constant indoors.

Dog & Cat Dander
Moderate · Constant
JFMAMJ JASOND

Pet dander is a constant indoor exposure. If you are sensitive, symptoms do not wait for a pollen season, and residue can linger in a home long after a pet leaves.
